About

The Yoruba people of Western Nigeria are known for their roasted plantain delicacy called bole. Bole can be made and served in a variety of inventive ways. While in areas like Port Harcourt and Bayelsa, it is eaten with roasted fish, yam, potato, or pepper sauce, it is typically served in the west with pepper sauce, groundnut, and palm oil. Bole is served with roasted chicken and pepper sauce in Akwa Ibom.
PLANTAIN is a great source of starch and energy is. It has a lot of fiber, which promotes regular bowel motions and lessens constipation. Additionally, it has a lot of folic acid as well as vitamins A, C, and B complex. Vitamins B and C are excellent antioxidants that support healthy body functioning and the regeneration of damaged tissues. Iron, magnesium, potassium, and calcium are among the important minerals that are abundant in plantains. Strong bones can form with the help of calcium.

When prepared properly, the local delicacy Bole (roasted plantain), which is currently used to refer to roasted plantain can be enjoyed with the either of the combinations of yam, groundnut or fish, and is a healthful and enjoyable meal.

Recipe

Ingredient Quantity/Measurement
Plantains 4-6 Seeds
Fish 4-6 Seeds
Pepper 4-6 Seeds
Palm Oil 1/4 cup
Onions 1 small size
Salt To taste
Groundnuts As much as desired

Preparation

  1. Pre-heat your oven grill at 170C until hot.
  2. Peel the plantains out of their coat and rub a little salt on them
  3. Place the peeled plantains on the grill rack and pop into the hot oven for about 15minutes. Make sure you turn the plantains over at intervals till done and then bring out and set aside
  4. For the sauce, heat the palm oil in a pot and add the chopped onions. Fry for a couple minutes then add the chopped peppes. Add some salt to taste. Fry for another 2 minutes while stirring.
  5. Turn off heat
